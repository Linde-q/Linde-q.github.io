除数算法的完整证明

Principle of Well-Ordering(良序原则).

Every nonempty subset of natural numbers contain a least element.

(Division Algorithm)

  • Given integers a and b, with b>0, there exist unique integers q and r, called quotient and remainder
    respectively, such that

$$
a = qb + r
$$

  • where $0 ≤ r < b$

$$
Proof
$$

The proof contains two parts: existence(存在性) and uniqueness(唯一性).

  • Existence of q and r.

Let

$S = \left\lbrace a − bk : k \in Z \ and\ a− bk \ge 0\right\rbrace$

It is easy to show that S is nonempty.

By the Principle of Well-Ordering, there is a smallest member $r ∈ S$, and $r = a − qb$.

Therefore, a = qb + r, r ≥ 0.

To show r < b ,Using proof by contradiction, let’s assume that r > b. Then we can find

$r’ = r - b = a - ( 1 + q )b < r$

This contradicts the fact that r is the smallest member in S. Therefore, we conclude that r < b.

  • Uniqueness of q and r.

Suppose there exist $r,r’,q$ and $q’$ such that

$a=bq+r,0\leq r<b$ and $a=bq’+r’,0\leq r’<b$.

Then $bq+r=bq’+r’.$

Assume that $r’>r$.From the last equation we have $b(a-q’)=r’-r$;therefore,b must divide $r’-r$ and $0\leq r’-r \leq r’<b$.This is possible only if $r’-r=0$.

Hence, we have proven the uniqueness of q and r.
